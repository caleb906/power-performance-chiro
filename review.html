03  /  Why story matters The conversion engine
The conversion engine

Traffic isn't the win.
Booked patients are.

Once someone lands on the page, they decide in about 8 seconds whether to scroll, click, or close the tab. Story is what makes that decision go your way. We use Donald Miller's StoryBrand framework: the customer is the hero, you're the guide, and the homepage is the moment they realize they have a plan.

The seven beats every homepage needs:

A Character Wants Something

Name who the visitor is and what they want. On PPC: "Out of pain. Staying that way."

And Has a Problem

External pain + internal frustration. "You shouldn't have to live around the pain."

Then Meets a Guide

Empathy first, authority second. "A chiropractor who actually gets it."

Who Gives Them a Plan

Three simple steps. Book Online → First Visit → Back to You.

And Calls Them to Action

Direct CTA in the hero, repeated near the close. No friction, one job.

That Ends in Success

Paint the future. "Back to the things you actually love."

That Helps Avoid Failure

FAQ removes the last objections that keep people from booking.

04  /  The tension SEO vs Story
The tension

SEO wants keywords.
Story wants humans.

SEO copy, left alone, ends up keyword-stuffed and lifeless. Story copy, left alone, ranks for nothing. Most chiropractic websites pick a side and lose half the battle. The trick is writing one page that does both jobs at once.

SEO-only
"Sports chiropractic care in Bend OR offering Active Release Technique, FAKTR Protocol, and Functional Rehabilitation for athletes."
Ranks for the keywords. Says nothing a human in pain wants to read. Visitors bounce.
Story-only
"You deserve to feel like yourself again."
Emotional. Beautiful. Google has no idea what you do or where you do it. Nobody finds it.
05  /  How we balanced it The fix
The fix

Story carries the page.
SEO lives in the structure.

The visible copy is written for the human in pain. The behind-the-scenes structure (titles, meta, headings, schema, image alt text) carries the keyword load. Visitors see story. Google sees signal. Same page.

Visible copy
Story
"Out of pain. Staying that way." "Three ways we get you back to yourself."
Page title + meta
SEO
"Sports Chiropractor in Bend, OR | Power Performance Chiropractic"
Schema markup
Both
Chiropractor schema with address, hours, area served, services, and 5-star rating.

The phrase "Bend's sports chiropractor" lives in the actual hero subhead. The phrase "Active Release," "FAKTR," "Winback Tecar" lives in image alt text, the FAQ, and the page schema. The visitor never has to read a list of acronyms. Google reads them anyway.

07  /  What's on the page now The build
The build

Nine sections.
One job each.

Every section is doing one thing. No filler.

Hero

Headline, subhead with location and audience, primary + secondary CTA, social proof (5.0 Google reviews).

Problem

Name the pain visitors actually feel. Stakes for not solving it.

Services (Value Prop)

Three outcomes the visitor walks away with. Modality names live in the small meta only.

Guide

Dr. Chandler. Empathy + authority. Real photo, real creds, real quote.

Plan

Book Online → First Visit → Back to You. Three steps, no mystery.

Reviews

Three Google reviews, 5.0 rating, link to the full Google profile.

Success

Paint the life on the other side. Local to Central Oregon.

FAQ

Answer the objections that keep people from clicking Book.

Final CTA

"Feel better. Stay that way." Book. Call. Done.
